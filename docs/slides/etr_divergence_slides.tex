\begin{frame}{Formal Model}

\small

The realized ETR in month $t$ is
\[
\tau_t \;=\; \frac{R_t}{I_t}
\]
where $R_t$ is total customs duties collected and $I_t$ is total import value.

\medskip

For a counterfactual statutory rate, decompose by product $p$ and country $c$:
\[
\tau^{\text{stat}}_t \;=\; \frac{\sum_p \sum_c w_{pc}(t)\,\tau_{pc}(t)}{\sum_p \sum_c w_{pc}(t)}
\]

\medskip

\textbf{Five tiers} are constructed by holding either the rates $\tau_{pc}$ or the weights $w_{pc}$ at counterfactual values:

\begin{tabular}{l l l}
\toprule
Tier & Rate $\tau_{pc}$        & Weight $w_{pc}$ \\
\midrule
\tier{S0} & USMCA at 2024 shares     & 2024 baseline \\
\tier{S1} & USMCA at H2-2025 shares  & 2024 baseline \\
\tier{S2} & USMCA at H2-2025 shares  & monthly $w_{pc}(t)$ \\
\tier{S3} & H2-2025 + non-USMCA pref & monthly $w_{pc}(t)$ \\
\tier{T}  & Treasury actual          & --- \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\hyperlink{appendix_rates}{\beamergotobutton{Authority stacking detail}}

\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Data sources}

\small

\begin{description}\setlength{\itemsep}{0.4em}
  \item[\textbf{Census IMDB}] HS10 $\times$ country $\times$ month, available through \emph{February 2026}. Provides both the monthly trade weights $w_{pc}(t)$ \emph{and} the cell-level Census-collected duties:
    \begin{itemize}\small
      \item \texttt{con\_val\_mo}: consumption value (importer-declared dollars at entry).
      \item \texttt{cal\_dut\_mo}: calculated duties (importer-declared duty amount at entry).
    \end{itemize}

  \item[\textbf{USITC DataWeb}] Per-(HS10 $\times$ country) USMCA program codes (S/S+). Source of the three USMCA claim-rate scenarios that scale $\tau_{pc}$.

  \item[\textbf{Tariff Rate Tracker}] (Budget Lab sibling repo): produces the cell-level rates $\tau_{pc}(t)$ from HTSUS + Chapter 99 parsing, with authority stacking and day-weighting across HTS revisions within the month.

  \item[\textbf{U.S. Treasury}] Monthly customs duties from the Monthly Treasury Statement / Daily Treasury Statement series. \alert{Reported aggregate-only -- not disaggregated by country or product.}
\end{description}

\vspace{0.3em}

\textbf{Connection to the formal model}: weights $w_{pc}$ from Census; rates $\tau_{pc}$ from Tariff Rate Tracker scaled by USMCA shares from DataWeb; realized $\tau_t = R_t/I_t$ from Treasury.

\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Two ways of decomposing by group}

\small

\begin{columns}[T]
\begin{column}{0.5\textwidth}
\textbf{(a) Shapley two-way decomposition}

\medskip

For partition $g$ (partner or product),
\[
\Delta S = \sum_g \left[\tfrac{1}{2}(R^0_g + R^1_g)(s^0_g - s^1_g)\right]
\]
\vspace{-1em}
\[
+ \sum_g \left[\tfrac{1}{2}(s^0_g + s^1_g)(R^0_g - R^1_g)\right]
\]

Splits each channel into:
\begin{itemize}\small
  \item \emph{between-group} (composition shift)
  \item \emph{within-group} (rate or mix shift inside the group)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{What it shows:} which groups carry how much of the channel \emph{after} share-weight adjustment.
\end{column}
\begin{column}{0.5\textwidth}
\textbf{(b) Average ETR within each group}

\medskip

For each group $g$, plot the per-group ETR series under each tier:
\[
R_g^{\text{tier}}(t) = \frac{\sum_{i \in g} w^{\text{tier}}_i \tau^{\text{tier}}_i}{\sum_{i \in g} w^{\text{tier}}_i}
\]

Each panel shows two lines; the visible spread is the channel's contribution from that group.

\textbf{What it shows:} the magnitude of each group's ETR \emph{within itself}, side-by-side at each tier.

\end{column}
\end{columns}

\begin{frame}{A2. Shapley two-way derivation}
\hypertarget{appendix_shapley}{}

\small

For aggregate $S(w) = \sum_g s_g(w) R_g(w)$ where $g$ partitions $i = (\text{HS10}, \text{cty})$ cells, with $R_g(w) = \sum_{i \in g} w_i r_i / \sum_{i \in g} w_i$ and $s_g(w) = \sum_{i \in g} w_i / \sum_i w_i$:

\medskip

A first-order Shapley decomposition of $\Delta S = S(w^1) - S(w^0)$ averages the two orderings of (share change, rate change):
\[
\Delta S = \tfrac{1}{2}\!\sum_g\!\left[(R_g^0 + R_g^1)(s_g^1 - s_g^0)\right] + \tfrac{1}{2}\!\sum_g\!\left[(s_g^0 + s_g^1)(R_g^1 - R_g^0)\right].
\]

\medskip

Validation: across both lenses (country, product) and across all months, the per-rung sum $\sum_g \text{between}_g + \text{within}_g$ matches the framework's \texttt{gap\_diversion} from \texttt{counterfactual\_ladder.dta} to within numerical noise. This is asserted in 03 Section B at runtime.

\medskip

Sign convention: positive contribution = positive contribution to $S^{\text{2024 wts}} - S^{\text{monthly wts}}$ at the fixed rate panel.

\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{A3. Rate panel construction}
\hypertarget{appendix_rates}{}

\small

Per (HS10 $\times$ country) cell, the tariff-rate-tracker (\texttt{src/06\_calculate\_rates.R}) produces \emph{per-revision} rates by stacking authority components:

\begin{enumerate}\small
  \item Authority components from HTSUS / Chapter 99 text: \texttt{rate\_232}, \texttt{rate\_ieepa\_recip}, \texttt{rate\_fent}, \texttt{rate\_301}, \texttt{rate\_s122}, base MFN.
  \item Metal-content scaling (S232): rate scaled by per-type metal share; IEEPA recip / S122 apply to the non-metal portion.
  \item Authority stacking: China-with-232 = $232 + \text{recip} \cdot \text{nonmetal} + \text{fent} + 301 + \text{s122} \cdot \text{nonmetal}$; CA/MX-with-232 = $232 + (\text{recip} + \text{fent} + \text{s122}) \cdot \text{nonmetal}$; without-232 = all authorities full.
  \item USMCA scaling at the H2-2025 average (or 2024 baseline / monthly empirical, depending on scenario). Auto/MHD parts use a 0.40 US-content rule.
  \item MFN exemption shares (HS2 $\times$ country) and IEEPA floor adjustments (10\% minimum for EU/JP/KR/CH).
\end{enumerate}

\medskip

Per-revision rates are then \alert{day-weighted across revisions within each month} in R section 3e to produce HS10 $\times$ country $\times$ month panels (one per USMCA scenario), so mid-month policy changes (e.g., Liberation Day on April 2, 2025) are correctly captured.

\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{A9. Limitations \& caveats}

\small

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{H2-2025 baseline is a stylized choice.} If USMCA claim rates continue to evolve post-Feb 2026, the S1/S2 panel anchors may need updating. The framework structure tolerates this — just rebuild \texttt{rate\_h2avg} from new tracker scenario snapshots.
  \item \textbf{Product groupings are policy-salient, not exhaustive.} The 9-group split groups copper (Ch 74) into ``Other Manufactured'' even though it became S232-relevant in June 2025. A finer split is straightforward.
  \item \textbf{Treasury timing.} The S4 $\to$ T gap mixes cash-vs-accrual, refunds, FTZ deferrals, and post-entry adjustments. Our framework does not separately attribute these; further work would require Treasury microdata.
  \item \textbf{S2 $\to$ S3 by-group decomposition is in development.} The country/product Shapley currently runs only for S1 $\to$ S2. Extending to other rungs is mechanical given the same helper infrastructure.
  \item \textbf{Pre-2025 historical comparison absent.} The framework can in principle be re-run on 2017--19 data; this is on the to-do list.
\end{itemize}

\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{A12. Feb 2026 timing sign-flip}
\hypertarget{appendix_signflip}{}

\small

\textbf{Observation.} The S3$-$T gap (which combines \emph{residual} S3$-$S4 and \emph{timing} S4$-$T) flipped negative in Feb 2026. \textbf{Interpretation.} Census-declared duties (S4) keep \emph{below} the cell-level statutory reconstruction (S3) -- that is the structural ``residual'' from specific-duty AVE failures, AD/CVD, and within-cell behavioral noise. But Treasury cash receipts (T) now \emph{exceed} Census-declared duties by a widening margin. Cumulatively (Feb 2025--Feb 2026), Treasury has collected \alert{\$10.5B more} than IMDB shows declared (\hyperlink{appendix_cumduty}{cumulative \$ figure}). Plausible drivers: ACH lag catch-up, post-entry adjustments, refund reversals, and FTZ deferrals being paid down.
