The following is a set of steps for a simple decomposition of actual and statutory Effective Tariff Rates. 

I want you to explore how much of this infrastructure you can build in Stata. 


Part one: Data

I want you to construct two subdirectories in the Data folder: raw/ and working/ 
The raw data pulls (however defined) will be placed in raw/ and the cleaned data will be placed in working/
When not space-prohibitive, the working data will be stored in CSVs. 

1. Trade flow data via USITC API https://www.usitc.gov/applications/dataweb/api/dataweb_query_api.html
- I want monthly values, January 2024 through February 2026
- Trade flow: Imports, for consumption 
- Classification system: HTS items 
- Measures: Customs Value, Dutiable Value, Calculated Duties, First Unit of quantity 
- Separately by country and commodities (HS10 codes)

2. Actual revenue data via HAVER (see tariff-impact-tracker/blob/main/R/tariff_impacts_work.R)
- Replicate the pull for: # 2.1 Tariff Revenue & Effective Rates
#   FTRU@GOVFIN  = Federal customs duties ($M, SAAR)
#   TMMCN@USINT  = Goods imports, customs value ($M, SAAR)

3. Daily ETRs by country and product, via tariff-rate-tracker
- Pull in daily rates by country and product from January 2025 through February 2026 
- Two versions: 
--> Baseline 
--> Alternative with 2024 USMCA shares (this will be what we start with). 
--> collapse to monthly level (average / max / min / end of month ETR by product and country) for both. 

Part two: analysis

First, merge together data (1) and (3) above. We want a dataset that is organized at the month X country X HS10 product level. For each row, we'll have the Census trade data, and the different ETRs derived from the daily ETR calculator. 

Second, calculate a few different values: 
- The implied monthly ETR via the census data: census_etr = Calculated Duties / Customs Value
- 2024 trade weights for product X countries (w_{cp,2024} = \sum_m custom_value_{cp,m} / \sum_{cp} \sum_m custom_value_{cp,m} )
- Monthly trade weights for product X countries (w_{cp,m} = custom_value_{cp,m} / \sum_{cp} custom_value_{cp,m} )
